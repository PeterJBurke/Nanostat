Help

Instructions:

On bootup, blue LED flashes until setup complete.

To run a sweep, go to SWEEP page and click button for sweep type. (See below for sweep type explanations.) During sweep, blue LED flashes (unless time per point less than 10 ms).

To set sweep parameters, goto setup page. Click a button to run a sweep. Sweep is plotted on webpage at end of sweep, and automatically downloaded.

To bias at a specific point and monitor voltages, goto panel page.

Zero bias: Minimum cell voltage with LMP91000 is 1% of 1.5V = 15 mV, gives slight discontinuity at origin.

Network:

If no WIFI connection is made with stored credentials, NanoStat goes into access point mode. Use the SSID "Nanostat" and enter credentials on website at 192.168.4.1.

Once connected, on a laptop goto http://nanostat.local. On an Android mobile device, you need the actual IP address of the nanostat from your router. Contact your network administrator to find this from the router.

Calibration:

To calibrate, run "Cal" with WE disconnected from RE/CE. (Short RE/CE). Calibrations saved to flash, persist after reboot.

Choosing TIA resistance (Trans-Impedance Amplifier):

Due to limits on the ADC of the ESP32, you need to ensure -0.75 V < VTIA < 1 V, where VTIA = RTIA * WE current.

Maximum # of points:

5000. Do not exceed.

Maximum cell voltage:

The LMP91000 can bias the cell up to 20% of the DAC output of the ESP32. The ESP32 DAC is very non-linear above 3 V, and cannot go above 3.3 V which is the power supply voltage. Therefore the cell voltage is limited to 3 V * 20% = 600 mV, in either direction.

Sweep modes:

CV = Cyclic voltammetry.
Bioanalytical Systems, Inc. tutorial on CV
NPV = Normal pulsed voltammetry.
Bioanalytical Systems, Inc. tutorial on NPV
SWV = Square wave voltammetry.
Bioanalytical Systems, Inc. tutorial on SWV
CA = Chronoamperometry.
Bioanalytical Systems, Inc. tutorial on CA
DC = Fixed DC bias.

Sets dc bias voltage, reads ADC with various # readings per point {1, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500, 1000}, gives avg and std deviation of ADC value in bits.

IV = IV curve.
Cal = Calibrate NanoStat.
